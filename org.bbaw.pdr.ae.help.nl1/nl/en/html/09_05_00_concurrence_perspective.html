Concurrence-perspective

The concurrence perspective opens in the same way as the identifier perspective. This perspective is also split into different sections, although this time four instead of two. With the concurrence perspective it is possible to find persons that are similar to another and then either mark them as concurrent or integrate the data of one person into the other. This function helps to avoid the overlap or doubling of data. For instance, two users could unknowingly enter the same person in the Archiv-Editor, and this function allows these two entries to be combined into one person. Or, it is possible that a person was entered with very little data and is then later identified as a person already existing in the Editor. This function exists to remove these kinds of double entries.

As already mentioned, the concurrence perspective has four sections. The person that has been selected from the tree and should be checked for double entries is displayed in the lower left hand box with an aspect-view. In the box above, you can search for persons that have overlapping characteristics. Checkmark the desired search criteria, such as name or date of birth, and click search. A list will appear with the overlapping persons (assuming that such persons exist). Double click on a person in the list to open them up in the lower right hand box, also in an aspect-view. The overlapping person’s information will also be displayed in the upper right hand box.

It is now possible to compare the overlapping data of the two persons that are display in the two lower (left and right) aspect-view boxes. If an actual concurrence is found, then there are three options: (1) marking both persons as concurrent (2) merging the data of the person on the right into the person on the left (3) merging the data of the person on the left into that of the right. These options are displayed on the three buttons which separate the upper and lower section of the perspective. Merging data means that one person receives all the aspects of the other and that the other person is deleted. If the persons should not be integrated, but the concurrence should be recorded (option one), simply click “Set Concurrence in both Persons”.
