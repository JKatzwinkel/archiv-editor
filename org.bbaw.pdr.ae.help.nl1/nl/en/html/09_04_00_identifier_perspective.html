Identifier-perspective

The first step is to open the identifier perspective. In the far upper right corner of the window lie two buttons. The right button looks like a text field and states, most likely, “Main-Pers...”, meaning that this perspective is currently active. If this is not the case, clicking here will open the main perspective. The other button (a colored square with a gold cross in the corner), allows further perspectives to be opened.

Click on this colored square and choose the option “Other...”, this opens the list of available perspectives. Select IdentifierPerspective and click “Ok”. Now the identifier perspective is open and the button in the upper right hand corner will read “IdentifierPers...”. The button reading “Maim-Perspective” might now be hidden, but can be shown again by clicking the small black arrow to the right of the box. If you want the perspectives that are open to be shown, simply drag the tab on which the perspective box is found, enlarging it and allowing all open perspectives to be displayed.

The identifier perspective is split into two sections. On the left side is an aspect-view, where objects can be opened and worked on, although with less room as usual. The right side contains the identifier search view . Here you can visit internet websites using a web address or bookmark, similar to an internet browser like Mozilla Firefox. The idea is that identifiers for a person can be searched for in the right hand identifier-perspective (the person must first be selected and displayed in the left hand miniature aspect-view). Identifiers like the PND can be determined and with the person-editor (see chapter...) attached as an external identifier.
